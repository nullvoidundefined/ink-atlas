# InkAtlas Master Plan

## 1. Vision

InkAtlas is a city-first tattoo discovery platform helping people find the right tattoo artist by style, neighborhood, budget, and availability.

InkAtlas should evolve into a hybrid of:
- Local editorial guide
- Searchable marketplace
- Map discovery engine

Initial rollout:
1. Portland
2. Seattle
3. Expand city by city

## 5. Supply Acquisition Strategy

### Data Acquisition & Source Policy

InkAtlas should not rely on scraping third-party platforms as its core data backbone. Use external sources for discovery and verification while building an independent canonical database.

### Recommended Sources
- Official APIs where available
- Public shop websites
- Public artist websites and booking pages
- Direct profile submissions
- Manual concierge research
- Public social links used for verification

### Avoid as Core Strategy
- Large-scale scraping of third-party maps platforms
- Copying third-party reviews or photos into permanent storage
- Building dependency on a single external platform

### Canonical Data Model
Maintain first-party records for:
- Artists
- Shops
- Locations
- Style tags
- Gallery metadata
- Availability
- Verification status
- Last reviewed timestamps

### Competitive Moat

External platforms help identify entities. InkAtlas wins through tattoo-specific metadata, curation, search quality, and claimed profiles.

## 7. Acquisition & Conversion Policy

### Founding Artist Program

#### Portland Launch
- First 100 artists: free for 12 months after activation
- Then legacy discounted pricing
- Second 100 artists: 50% off for 12 months
- Then full pricing

### Activation Requirements
- Claimed profile
- 12+ images
- Style tags
- Booking/contact method
- Availability status

### Purpose

The first cohorts are growth cohorts, not revenue cohorts.

They buy:
- Density
- Trust
- SEO inventory
- Referrals
- Marketplace credibility

## 8. Editorial Discovery Layer

### Example Content

- 18 Essential Tattoo Artists in Seattle
